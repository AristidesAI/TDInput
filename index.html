Ideas

What you can do with it

♥ Heart rate → generative art

Map your BPM to a reaction diffusion system, fluid simulation, or particle emitter. The piece breathes with the viewer.

↻ Phone tilt → camera control

Orbit around a 3D scene by tilting your wrist. Use it as a wireless controller during a live set.

☁ Acceleration → mesh displacement

Move fast, the surface breaks apart. Hold still, it settles. Interactive installations where the audience drives the visuals.

◈ Compass → color palette

Map compass heading to hue. North is blue, south is red. Walk in a circle, the spectrum cycles.

◎ Digital Crown → timeline

Scroll through keyframes, blend morph targets, or dial a parameter without touching a mouse.

▲ Altitude → environment

Walk upstairs, the fog lifts. Walk down, it closes in. Architecture drives the visuals in multi-level installations.

⟳ Gyroscope → particle emission

Spin your wrist, particles scatter. Stop, they collect. Rotation rate as emission intensity.

☷ Multiple phones → multi-performer

Each person controls a different layer. One wrist drives rotation, another phone drives color, someone's heart rate drives intensity.

♫ Wireless live set controller

Leave the laptop on stage. Control parameters from the crowd or from across the room.

⚡ Rapid sensor prototyping

30 channels at 50 Hz, ready to wire. Faster than setting up an Arduino every time you want to test an interaction.

Getting started

Setup

Takes about 30 seconds. iPhone and Mac on the same Wi-Fi.

1

Open TDInput on your iPhone. Enter your Mac's local IP address and port 9000.

2

In TouchDesigner, place an OSC In CHOP. Set the port to 9000.

3

Toggle Stream iPhone Sensors on in the app.

4

Channels appear in TD. Wire them into your project.

For Apple Watch: open TDInput on your watch and tap Start. Watch channels appear alongside phone channels.

Data

Privacy

TDInput collects no data. No analytics, no tracking, no cloud. All sensor data stays on your local network between your devices. Full privacy policy.
